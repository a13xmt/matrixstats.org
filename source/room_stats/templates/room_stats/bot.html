15.05.18 The bot is currently disabled
We were sad to know that the bot presence may impact homeserver performance in some cases. Until the situation would be solved, the bot will be disabled. If your server had strange performance issues for the last week (starting 7th May), please, consider that it was the case.

Details:
The bot performed two kind of requests: regular GET /sync requests with 5-10 min interval and GET /publicRooms requests with 3hr interval. The /sync ones seems to overload the server in cases a lot of rooms was joined previously. This was resulted in heavy RAM usage and a lot of 5xx errors for users for at least 2 huge community servers (with a chances to even more).

We are sorry for any inconvience that may occured in result of this event. The providen feedback from the community was very valuable, and we expect not to repeat some unintential abuses in the future.

Additional notes:
We are also stopping the bot from joining the rooms compeletly. While it was the experemental feature, that was intended to highlight active rooms for our users, it received a lot of controversal feedback, and it cannot be continued in the current form. All the rooms joined would be leaved for the next few days. The bot presence would be controlled by room members (i.e by direct invites), and not by the fact that the room presented in the public catalog.

Currently, the bot are still performing GET /publicRooms requests once in 3hr for each known public homeserver. This shouldn't affect homeserver performance in any way, but if it do, please, contact me at #matrixstats:matrix.org.


Some outdated info that would be changed:

Who are you?

Hi. I'm matrixstats bot. My destiny is to explore new homeservers and rooms. When I find a new homeserver, I register on it and join the rooms. Then I obtain statistical data and push it to catalog.

Statistical data?

For joined rooms, we collect number of active users and messages. The information is used to highlight active rooms, so users can find it more easily. There is no such information presented in the official API.

Is it safe?

Depends. The public rooms are opened to the world by default, so the information may leak in many ways. The bot is just a member, neither more nor less. It can do anything your members does. It's up to you to decide can it be safe or not.

What information is collected?

We need to accumulate events ids and mxids for counting and deduplicating events. This information is stored up for a month and then collapsed into the single numbers. We don't collect anything expect event ids and mxids.

Anything else?

The bot is currently in the alpha stage. We expect it to cover the whole network one day.
The information about joined homeservers is stored here.
